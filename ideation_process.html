Ideation Process

From user research insights to three product concepts — IOE 431 Team 8

Step 1: Key Pain Points from User Research

We synthesized our survey (19 responses) and interviews (3 participants) into 5 core user needs.

Scattered & Unreliable Info

Career information is fragmented across Google, Reddit, friends — hard to compare and often biased.

Survey: 80% rely on friends; Interviews: P1, P2

No "Real Feel" of Jobs

Students can't experience what a job actually looks like day-to-day before committing to a career path.

Interview: P1 "I have no idea what an IOE job looks like"

Skills ≠ Career Language

Students know their courses but can't translate academic strengths into professional skill keywords or roles.

Survey: 66% unsure of ideal career; Interview: P3

No Long-Term Visibility

Students can't visualize how their choices today impact salary, promotions, and career growth in 5–10 years.

Interview: P2 "I don't know what industry wants"

Decision Anxiety

Social media and job market uncertainty create anxiety. Students want data-backed confidence, not opinions.

Interview: P1 "Social media only creates anxiety"

Step 2: 10+10 Ideation Method

Each team member generated 10 ideas individually, then we collaboratively built 10 more as a group.

How We Did It

1

Individual Round

Each of the 6 team members silently wrote 10 ideas on sticky notes (60 total ideas)

2

Affinity Clustering

We grouped the 60 ideas into themes on a shared board and identified recurring patterns

3

Group Round

We built 10 more ideas by combining and evolving the strongest clusters

4

Dot Voting → 3 Concepts

Each member voted on their top 3; we converged on the 3 highest-voted concepts

Step 3: Three Product Concepts

Each concept directly addresses one or more pain points identified in our user research.

Feature 1: "Day in the Life" Simulator

Experiential Learning

Users select a career and experience a realistic, AI-generated workday timeline — complete with tasks, meetings, and a hands-on mini-game challenge.

Pain points addressed
No real feel of jobs Decision anxiety Low-friction exploration
View Prototype →

Feature 2: AI Skill Decoder

Skill-to-Career Translation

Users input favorite courses and self-described strengths. AI translates academic knowledge into professional skills and matches them to real career roles.

Pain points addressed
Skills ≠ career language Scattered info Personalized roadmap
View Prototype →

Feature 3: Interactive Career Roadmap

Long-Term Visualization

Users enter their profile (degree, skills, experience) and get a dynamic promotion timeline with salary projections that update when they add certifications.

Pain points addressed
No long-term visibility Decision anxiety Trustworthy signals

Step 4: Concept–Need Mapping

How each concept maps to the 5 design requirements from our user research.

Design Requirement Feature 1: Simulator Feature 2: Skill Decoder Feature 3: Roadmap
1. Trustworthy, industry-sourced insights ✓ Real task data ~ Skill database ✓ Market salary data
2. One-stop comparison ✓ Compare daily routines ✓ Compare matched roles ~ Single-track view
3. Personalized roadmap ~ Role-based only ✓ Based on user input ✓ Fully personalized
4. Bias control (multiple viewpoints) ✓ AI-generated neutral ✓ Data-driven matching ✓ Market data-backed
5. Low-friction exploration ✓ Tap & explore ✓ Quick course selection ~ Requires profile setup
